# RBI Monetary Policy and Guidelines

## Repo Rate and Interest Rate Framework

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) sets the repo rate, which is the rate at which commercial banks borrow money from the RBI. As of 2024, the repo rate is 6.50%. This rate directly influences the interest rates that banks charge on loans.

### Key Rates Set by RBI
- **Repo Rate**: 6.50% — The rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks
- **Reverse Repo Rate**: 3.35% — The rate at which RBI borrows from commercial banks
- **Bank Rate**: 6.75% — The rate at which RBI provides long-term funds to banks
- **CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio)**: 4.50% — Percentage of deposits banks must keep with RBI
- **SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio)**: 18% — Percentage of deposits banks must maintain in approved securities

## Impact on Borrowers
When the RBI increases the repo rate, banks' borrowing costs increase, and they pass this on to consumers through higher EMIs on floating rate loans. Conversely, when the repo rate decreases, EMIs tend to decrease.

### MCLR (Marginal Cost of Funds Based Lending Rate)
Banks set their lending rates based on MCLR, which is linked to the repo rate. Most banks offer home loans at MCLR + spread. The spread is determined by the borrower's credit profile and the type of loan.

## Digital Lending Guidelines (2022)
RBI issued comprehensive guidelines for digital lending in September 2022:
- All loan disbursals must go directly to borrower's bank account
- Recovery agents must follow ethical practices
- Loan agreements must clearly state annual percentage rate (APR)
- Borrowers must be given a cooling-off period
- No automatic increase in credit limits without explicit consent
- All digital lending apps must disclose the name of the NBFC/bank providing the loan

## Priority Sector Lending
RBI mandates that banks must allocate 40% of their lending to priority sectors:
- Agriculture: 18% of total lending
- Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs)
- Education loans
- Housing loans up to certain limits
- Loans to weaker sections

## Consumer Protection
- Banks must provide a Key Fact Statement (KFS) before loan disbursement
- Foreclosure charges cannot be levied on floating rate loans
- Banks must reset interest rates on floating rate loans at least once every 3 months under the external benchmark lending rate (EBLR) regime
- Loan agreements must be available in the language understood by the borrower

## RBI Consumer Education
The RBI encourages financial literacy and consumer awareness. Borrowers should:
- Compare interest rates across multiple banks
- Understand the difference between fixed and floating rates
- Read the fine print in loan agreements
- Check all charges — processing fees, prepayment penalties, late payment fees
- Verify that the lending institution is registered with RBI

# Insurance Guide — India

## Life Insurance

### Types of Life Insurance

#### 1. Term Insurance (Recommended)
- **Pure protection plan** — pays sum assured only on death during policy term
- **Cheapest** form of life insurance
- No maturity benefit (no payout if you survive the term)
- Example: ₹1 crore cover for 30-year-old male = ~₹700-900/month
- **Best for**: Primary earner of the family

| Company | ₹1 Cr Cover (30yr male, 30yr term) | Claim Settlement Ratio |
|---------|--------------------------------------|----------------------|
| LIC | ~₹1,100/month | 98.6% |
| HDFC Life | ~₹800/month | 98.5% |
| ICICI Pru | ~₹750/month | 97.8% |
| Max Life | ~₹850/month | 99.5% |
| Tata AIA | ~₹780/month | 98.9% |

#### 2. Whole Life Insurance
- Covers entire lifetime (up to 99 years)
- Higher premiums than term insurance
- Has a savings/investment component
- **Not recommended for most people** — returns are low compared to term + mutual fund combo

#### 3. ULIP (Unit Linked Insurance Plan)
- Combines insurance with mutual fund investment
- Higher charges (mortality, fund management, administration)
- 5-year lock-in period
- **Not recommended** — Better to buy term insurance + invest separately in mutual funds

#### 4. Endowment Plans
- Insurance + savings combination
- Guaranteed returns but very low (4-5%)
- **Not recommended** — PPF gives better guaranteed returns with tax benefits

### How Much Life Insurance Do You Need?
**Thumb Rule**: 10-15 times annual income
- Cover all outstanding loans (home loan, car loan, etc.)
- Future expenses (children's education, marriage)
- Family's living expenses for 15-20 years
- Subtract existing savings and investments

### Tax Benefits
- **Section 80C**: Premium paid up to ₹1.5 lakh/year
- **Section 10(10D)**: Maturity/death benefit tax-free (conditions apply)

## Health Insurance

### Why Health Insurance is Essential
- Average hospitalization cost in India: ₹2-5 lakh
- Cancer treatment: ₹10-25 lakh
- Heart surgery: ₹3-8 lakh
- Without insurance, a single medical emergency can wipe out years of savings

### Types of Health Insurance

#### 1. Individual Health Insurance
- Covers a single person
- Premium based on age, health, city, and sum insured
- Typical cover: ₹5-50 lakh

#### 2. Family Floater
- Single policy covering entire family (self, spouse, children, parents)
- Sum insured shared among all members
- More economical than individual policies for young families

#### 3. Top-Up / Super Top-Up
- Kicks in after a deductible (base insurance) is exhausted
- Very affordable way to increase coverage
- Example: ₹10 lakh super top-up with ₹5 lakh deductible = ~₹3,000-5,000/year

### Recommended Health Insurance Cover by Age
| Age Group | Recommended Cover | Approximate Premium (Individual) |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------------------|
| 25-30 | ₹10-15 lakh | ₹5,000-8,000/year |
| 30-40 | ₹15-25 lakh | ₹8,000-15,000/year |
| 40-50 | ₹25-50 lakh | ₹15,000-30,000/year |
| 50-60 | ₹25-50 lakh + Super Top-up | ₹30,000-60,000/year |
| 60+ | ₹50 lakh + Super Top-up | ₹60,000-1,20,000/year |

### Key Features to Look For
1. **No Room Rent Capping** — Avoid policies that limit room rent
2. **No Co-payment** — Choose 0% co-payment if possible
3. **Restoration Benefit** — Sum insured restored if exhausted during the year
4. **No Claim Bonus** — Sum insured increases by 10-50% each claim-free year
5. **Cashless Network** — Large network of hospitals for cashless treatment
6. **Waiting Period** — Check for pre-existing disease waiting period (usually 2-4 years)

### Tax Benefits
- **Section 80D**: Premium up to ₹25,000/year for self and family
- **Section 80D (Parents)**: Additional ₹25,000 for parents (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
- **Preventive Health Check-up**: Up to ₹5,000 within 80D limit

## Risk Assessment for Insurance Decisions
- **Underinsured Risk**: Having too little coverage can be as bad as no coverage
- **Over-insured Risk**: Paying high premiums for more coverage than needed
- **Claim Rejection Risk**: Not disclosing pre-existing conditions leads to claim rejection
- **Policy Lapse Risk**: Missing premium payments can void the policy
- **Inflation Risk**: ₹10 lakh cover today may not be enough 10 years later — buy top-ups as needed

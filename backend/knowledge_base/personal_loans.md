# Personal Loan Guide — India

## Overview
A personal loan is an unsecured loan that can be used for any purpose — medical emergency, wedding, travel, debt consolidation, home renovation, etc. Since it's unsecured, interest rates are higher than home loans.

## Eligibility
- Age: 21-60 years (salaried), 21-65 (self-employed)
- Minimum salary: ₹15,000-25,000/month depending on city
- Employment: Minimum 1-2 years experience
- CIBIL Score: 750+ for best rates (minimum 700)
- Loan amount: ₹50,000 to ₹40 lakh (up to ₹75 lakh for premium customers)
- Tenure: 1-5 years (up to 7 years at some banks)

## When to Take a Personal Loan
### Good Reasons
- Medical emergency when no health insurance or insufficient coverage
- Debt consolidation — replacing multiple high-interest debts with one lower-rate loan
- Home renovation that increases property value
- Education expenses not covered by education loans

### Bad Reasons — Avoid
- Lifestyle expenses (vacation, gadgets, luxury purchases)
- Investing in stock market or cryptocurrency (high risk)
- When you're already over-leveraged (total EMIs > 50% of income)
- When you have insufficient emergency fund

## Risk Factors
- **High Interest Rates**: 11%-26% is significantly higher than secured loans
- **Debt Trap**: Easy availability can lead to multiple loans
- **Credit Score Impact**: Late payments severely damage CIBIL score
- **Prepayment Penalty**: Some lenders charge 2-5% for early closure
- **Processing Fees**: Non-refundable even if loan is not disbursed

## Tips
1. Borrow only what you absolutely need
2. Keep total EMI obligations under 40% of net monthly income
3. Compare rates from at least 3 lenders
4. Read the fine print — check for hidden charges
5. Consider balance transfer if you find a lower rate later
6. Set up auto-debit for EMI to avoid late payment penalties

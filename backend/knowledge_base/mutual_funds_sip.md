# Mutual Fund and SIP — Detailed Guide

## What is a Mutual Fund?
A mutual fund is a professionally managed investment vehicle that pools money from multiple investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities.

## How SIP Works
Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly/weekly) in a mutual fund scheme.

### Benefits of SIP
1. **Rupee Cost Averaging**: Buy more units when price is low, fewer when high
2. **Power of Compounding**: Returns generate returns over time
3. **Discipline**: Automates investing behavior
4. **Flexibility**: Start with as low as ₹500/month
5. **No need to time market**: Regular investments smooth out volatility

## Types of Mutual Funds — Detailed

### By Structure
- **Open-ended**: Can buy/sell anytime at current NAV
- **Close-ended**: Fixed maturity, listed on exchange
- **Interval**: Open for purchase/redemption at specific intervals

## How to Choose a Mutual Fund
1. **Define your goal**: Retirement, child education, house purchase, wealth creation
2. **Determine time horizon**: <3 years (debt), 3-5 years (hybrid), 5+ years (equity)
3. **Assess risk tolerance**: Conservative, moderate, or aggressive
4. **Check fund performance**: Compare with benchmark AND category peers over 3/5/10 years
5. **Look at expense ratio**: Lower is better. Index funds: 0.1-0.5%, Active: 0.5-2.5%
6. **Check fund manager track record**: Experience and consistent performance
7. **Avoid NFOs**: New Fund Offers have no track record — stick to established funds

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Chasing past returns**: High recent returns may not sustain
2. **Too many funds**: 4-6 funds is optimal. More adds complexity without benefit
3. **Stopping SIP during market crash**: This is the WORST time to stop — you're buying cheap
4. **Not reviewing portfolio annually**: Rebalance if any asset class drifts 10%+ from target
5. **Ignoring expense ratio**: 1% higher expense ratio can reduce wealth by 20% over 20 years
6. **Switching funds frequently**: Generates tax and exit loads. Stick to plan for at least 3 years

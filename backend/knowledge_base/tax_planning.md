# Tax Planning Guide — India (FY 2024-25)

## Tax Regime Options

### Old Tax Regime (With Deductions)
| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|------------|---------|
| Up to ₹2.5 lakh | Nil |
| ₹2.5-5 lakh | 5% |
| ₹5-10 lakh | 20% |
| Above ₹10 lakh | 30% |

### New Tax Regime (Default from FY 2023-24)
| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|------------|---------|
| Up to ₹3 lakh | Nil |
| ₹3-7 lakh | 5% |
| ₹7-10 lakh | 10% |
| ₹10-12 lakh | 15% |
| ₹12-15 lakh | 20% |
| Above ₹15 lakh | 30% |

Standard deduction of ₹75,000 available in new regime.

### Which Regime to Choose?
- **New Regime** is better if your total deductions are less than ~₹3.75 lakh
- **Old Regime** is better if you have significant deductions (HRA, 80C, 80D, home loan)
- Calculate under both and choose the one with lower tax

## Key Tax-Saving Sections

### Section 80C (Up to ₹1.5 lakh) — Old Regime Only
| Investment | Lock-in | Risk | Returns |
|-----------|---------|------|---------|
| PPF | 15 years | Zero | 7.1% |
| ELSS Mutual Fund | 3 years | Moderate-High | 10-15% |
| 5-year Tax-saving FD | 5 years | Very Low | 6-7% |
| NPS (80CCD(1)) | Till 60 | Low-Moderate | 8-10% |
| Life Insurance Premium | Annual | N/A | N/A |
| EPF (Employee contribution) | Till retirement | Zero | 8.25% |
| SSY (Sukanya Samriddhi) | 21 years | Zero | 8.2% |
| NSC | 5 years | Zero | 7.7% |
| Home Loan Principal | N/A | N/A | N/A |
| ULIP | 5 years | Varies | Varies |

### Section 80CCD(1B) — Additional ₹50,000
- NPS contribution over and above Section 80C limit
- Available in old regime only

### Section 80D — Health Insurance
- Self + Family: Up to ₹25,000
- Parents: Additional ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior)
- Preventive health check-up: ₹5,000 within limits

### Section 24(b) — Home Loan Interest
- Up to ₹2 lakh for self-occupied property
- No limit for let-out property

### Section 80E — Education Loan Interest
- Full interest amount (no cap) for up to 8 years
- Available for self, spouse, children

### Section 80G — Donations
- 50% or 100% deduction depending on organization
- Must be to registered charitable institutions

## Tax-Efficient Investment Strategy by Age

### Age 20-30 (Wealth Building Phase)
1. Max out ELSS (₹1.5L under 80C) — Best for growth + tax saving
2. Start NPS early for additional 80CCD(1B) benefit
3. Get term insurance + health insurance (80C + 80D)
4. Stay in new regime if you have few deductions

### Age 30-45 (Family Building Phase)
1. Home loan gives massive tax benefit (80C + 24b)
2. Increase health insurance to cover family
3. PPF for risk-free portion of 80C
4. Start children's education planning (SSY for daughters)
5. Likely old regime is better with multiple deductions

### Age 45-60 (Pre-Retirement Phase)
1. Max NPS contributions for additional tax benefit
2. Consider Voluntary Provident Fund (VPF) for safe returns
3. Diversify between ELSS, PPF, NPS for 80C
4. Ensure adequate health insurance cover

## Common Tax Mistakes to Avoid
1. Not submitting investment proofs to employer on time
2. Investing in insurance products (endowment/ULIP) just for tax saving
3. Ignoring Section 80D — health insurance is essential anyway
4. Not keeping receipts for donations (Section 80G)
5. Filing ITR late — penalty of ₹5,000 under Section 234F
6. Not declaring all income sources (FD interest, capital gains, rental income)

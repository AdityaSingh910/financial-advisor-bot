# Education Loan Guide — India

## Overview
Education loans help students finance higher education in India or abroad. They cover tuition fees, hostel charges, examination fees, books, equipment, and travel expenses.

## Government Schemes
### Central Sector Interest Subsidy Scheme
- Full interest subsidy during moratorium period (course + 1 year)
- For students from families with annual income up to ₹4.5 lakh
- Available for technical/professional courses

### Vidyalakshmi Portal
- Single platform to apply for education loans from multiple banks
- Government initiative to simplify education loan process
- Apply at www.vidyalakshmi.co.in

## Loan Coverage
### Expenses Covered
- Tuition and hostel fees
- Examination and library fees
- Books, equipment, and instruments
- Travel expenses (for study abroad)
- Caution deposit and building fund
- Computer/laptop purchase (if required by course)

### Not Covered
- Donation or capitation fees
- Coaching fees for competitive exams
- Expenses already covered by scholarship

## Repayment
- **Moratorium Period**: Course duration + 1 year (no EMI during this period)
- **Repayment Period**: 5-15 years after moratorium
- **Interest during moratorium**: Accrues and added to principal (simple interest for some banks)
- **Tax Benefit**: Section 80E — Full interest deduction for up to 8 years (no cap on amount)

## Eligibility
- Indian citizen
- Secured admission to recognized institution
- Courses: Graduation, post-graduation, professional courses, PhD
- Co-applicant required (parent/guardian)
- No minimum income requirement for student

## Risk Assessment for Education Loans
- **Course Quality Risk**: Choose courses with good placement records
- **Employment Risk**: Research job market demand for your chosen field
- **Currency Risk**: For abroad loans, EMI can increase with currency depreciation
- **Interest Accumulation**: Interest during moratorium increases total cost significantly
- **Over-borrowing**: Borrow only the gap between total cost and available funds

## Tips
1. Apply well in advance — loan processing takes 2-4 weeks
2. Compare at least 3 banks and 2 NBFCs
3. Consider partial interest payment during moratorium to reduce total cost
4. Keep all academic documents organized
5. Start repaying early if you start earning during the course
6. Claim Section 80E tax benefit every year during repayment

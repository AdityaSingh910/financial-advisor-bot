# Credit Score (CIBIL Score) — Complete Guide

## What is a CIBIL Score?
CIBIL Score is a 3-digit numeric summary (300-900) of your credit history, assigned by TransUnion CIBIL. It is the most widely used credit score in India and is checked by banks and NBFCs before approving any loan or credit card.

## Score Ranges and What They Mean
| Score Range | Rating | Loan Approval Chances |
|------------|--------|----------------------|
| 750-900 | Excellent | Very High — Best interest rates |
| 700-749 | Good | High — Competitive rates available |
| 650-699 | Fair | Moderate — Higher interest rates |
| 600-649 | Poor | Low — Limited options, very high rates |
| 300-599 | Very Poor | Very Low — Most banks will reject |
| -1 | No History | No credit history — Need to build |

## Factors That Affect Your Credit Score

### 1. Payment History (35% weightage)
- **Most important factor**
- Late EMI or credit card payments severely damage your score
- Even one 30-day late payment can drop your score by 50-100 points
- Defaults and write-offs stay on your report for 7 years
- **Tip**: Set up auto-debit for all EMIs and credit card minimum payments

### 2. Credit Utilization Ratio (30% weightage)
- The percentage of your available credit limit that you're using
- Keep utilization below 30% of total credit limit
- Example: If your credit card limit is ₹1 lakh, keep outstanding below ₹30,000
- **Tip**: Request credit limit increase to reduce utilization ratio without spending more

### 3. Credit Age (15% weightage)
- Average age of all your credit accounts
- Older accounts are better for your score
- **Tip**: Don't close your oldest credit card, even if you don't use it much

### 4. Credit Mix (10% weightage)
- Having a mix of secured (home loan, car loan) and unsecured (credit card, personal loan) credit
- Shows lenders you can handle different types of credit responsibly
- **Tip**: Don't take unnecessary loans just for mix — it's only 10% weightage

### 5. New Credit Inquiries (10% weightage)
- Each loan/card application triggers a "hard inquiry" on your report
- Multiple inquiries in a short period signal credit hunger
- **Tip**: Don't apply for multiple loans/cards at the same time. Rate-shop within a 14-day window for same type of loan — counted as single inquiry

## How to Improve Your Credit Score
1. **Pay all EMIs and credit card bills on time** — Most impactful action
2. **Reduce credit card utilization** below 30%
3. **Don't close old credit cards** — Preserve credit age
4. **Limit new credit applications** — Each one causes a hard inquiry
5. **Check your CIBIL report annually** — Dispute any errors at www.cibil.com
6. **Become an authorized user** on a family member's old, well-maintained card
7. **Use a secured credit card** if you have no credit history
8. **Set up auto-debit** for minimum due to avoid accidental late payments
9. **Don't be a loan guarantor** unless absolutely necessary — their defaults affect your score
10. **Avoid settling loans** — "Settled" status is worse than "Closed" on your report

## Impact of Credit Score on Loan Interest Rates
| Score | Home Loan Rate Impact | Personal Loan Rate Impact |
|-------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| 800+ | Base rate (8.5%) | Best rate (10.5%) |
| 750-799 | +0.1-0.3% | +1-2% |
| 700-749 | +0.3-0.5% | +2-4% |
| 650-699 | +0.5-1.0% | +4-8% |
| Below 650 | May be rejected | Likely rejected |

A difference of just 50 points can mean paying lakhs more in interest over a loan's lifetime.

## How to Check Your CIBIL Score
- **Free**: Once per year at www.cibil.com
- **Paid (CIBIL subscription)**: ₹550/year for unlimited checks + alerts
- **Through banks**: Many banks show CIBIL score in their mobile apps (SBI, HDFC, ICICI)
- **Soft inquiry**: Checking your own score does NOT affect it

## Common Myths Debunked
- ❌ "Checking my own score reduces it" → ✅ Self-checks are soft inquiries, no impact
- ❌ "I don't need credit score if I don't take loans" → ✅ You might need it unexpectedly
- ❌ "High salary means high credit score" → ✅ Score depends on credit behavior, not income
- ❌ "Closing a credit card improves score" → ✅ It often hurts (reduces available credit, reduces credit age)
- ❌ "Paying minimum due is enough" → ✅ It avoids late fee but interest accrues and utilization stays high

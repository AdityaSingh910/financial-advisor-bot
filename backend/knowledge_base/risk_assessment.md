# Financial Risk Assessment Framework

## Risk Categories in Personal Finance

### 1. Market Risk
The possibility that investment returns may be lower than expected due to market fluctuations.

**Risk Level by Investment Type:**
| Investment | Market Risk | Factors |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Equity Mutual Funds | High | Market cycles, sector performance, global events |
| Debt Mutual Funds | Low-Moderate | Interest rate changes, credit risk |
| Fixed Deposits | Negligible | Bank default (covered by DICGC up to ₹5L) |
| PPF/EPF | Zero | Government guaranteed |
| Gold | Moderate | Global demand, USD/INR, geopolitics |
| Real Estate | Moderate-High | Location, demand-supply, regulatory changes |

### 2. Credit Risk (for Borrowers)
Risk of financial distress due to over-borrowing or adverse events.

**Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Assessment:**
| DTI Ratio | Risk Level | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Below 20% | Low | Comfortable — can consider additional borrowing if needed |
| 20-35% | Moderate | Manageable — avoid new debt unless necessary |
| 35-50% | High | Stressed — focus on debt reduction, no new loans |
| Above 50% | Critical | Over-leveraged — seek debt restructuring or counseling |

**How to calculate DTI:** Total Monthly EMIs ÷ Net Monthly Income × 100

### 3. Inflation Risk
Risk that returns don't keep up with inflation, eroding real purchasing power.

**Real Returns = Nominal Returns - Inflation Rate**

| Investment | Nominal Returns | Real Returns (at 6% inflation) |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Savings Account | 3-4% | -2 to -3% (NEGATIVE) |
| FD | 6-7.5% | 0 to 1.5% |
| PPF | 7.1% | 1.1% |
| Equity MF (long-term) | 12-15% | 6-9% |
| Gold (long-term) | 8-10% | 2-4% |

### 4. Liquidity Risk
Risk of not being able to access money when needed.

| Investment | Liquidity | Penalty for Early Access |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| Savings Account | Immediate | None |
| Liquid MF | T+1 day | None (if >7 days) |
| FD | Same day | 0.5-1% rate penalty |
| PPF | Partial after 7 years | Cannot access fully |
| NPS | After 60 (limited before) | 20% lump sum penalty |
| Real Estate | Weeks to months | Agent fees, registration |
| ELSS | After 3 years | Cannot access before |

### 5. Interest Rate Risk (for Existing Borrowers)
Risk that floating rate loan EMIs increase due to rate hikes.

**Impact Analysis (₹50 Lakh Home Loan, 20 years):**
| Rate Change | New EMI | Monthly Increase | Total Extra Over Tenure |
|------------|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| +0.5% | +₹1,500 | ₹1,500 | ₹3.6 lakh |
| +1.0% | +₹3,000 | ₹3,000 | ₹7.2 lakh |
| +1.5% | +₹4,600 | ₹4,600 | ₹11.0 lakh |
| +2.0% | +₹6,200 | ₹6,200 | ₹14.9 lakh |

## Risk Profiling Framework

### Conservative Investor
- **Age**: Typically 50+ or any age with low risk tolerance
- **Goal**: Capital preservation
- **Recommended Mix**: 80% debt (FD, PPF, debt MF) + 15% gold + 5% equity
- **Expected Returns**: 6-8%

### Moderate Investor
- **Age**: Typically 35-50
- **Goal**: Balanced growth with stability
- **Recommended Mix**: 50% equity + 35% debt + 10% gold + 5% cash
- **Expected Returns**: 8-12%

### Aggressive Investor
- **Age**: Typically 20-35 with long investment horizon
- **Goal**: Maximum wealth creation
- **Recommended Mix**: 70-80% equity + 10-15% debt + 5-10% gold
- **Expected Returns**: 12-15%

## Common Financial Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Job Loss | Can't pay EMIs, lifestyle disruption | Emergency fund (6-12 months), income protection insurance |
| Medical Emergency | Can wipe out savings | Health insurance (₹15-25 lakh minimum cover) |
| Death of Earner | Family financial crisis | Term insurance (10-15x annual income) |
| Disability | Income stops, expenses increase | Critical illness + disability cover |
| Inflation | Savings lose value | Invest in equity for long-term goals |
| Market Crash | Portfolio value drops | Asset allocation, avoid panic selling, SIP continues |
| Fraud/Scam | Financial loss | Use official apps, verify before investing, don't share OTP |

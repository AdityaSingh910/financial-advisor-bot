# Banking Services and Digital Banking — India

## Types of Bank Accounts

### Savings Account
- **Purpose**: Everyday transactions and savings
- **Interest Rate**: 2.70% - 7.00% (varies by bank)
- **Minimum Balance**: ₹0 (Jan Dhan) to ₹10,000 (private banks)
- **Features**: Debit card, net banking, UPI, mobile banking

### Current Account
- **Purpose**: Business transactions
- **Interest Rate**: 0% (no interest on current accounts)
- **Minimum Balance**: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000 typically
- **Features**: Higher transaction limits, overdraft facility

### Fixed Deposit (FD)
- **Purpose**: Guaranteed returns on lump sum
- **Interest Rate**: 6.50% - 7.50%
- **Lock-in**: Flexible tenure (7 days to 10 years)
- **Premature withdrawal**: Allowed with penalty

### Recurring Deposit (RD)
- **Purpose**: Disciplined monthly savings
- **Interest Rate**: Similar to FD
- **Minimum Deposit**: ₹100-₹500/month
- **Tenure**: 6 months to 10 years

## Digital Banking and UPI

### UPI (Unified Payments Interface)
- **Developed by**: NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India)
- **Transaction Limit**: ₹1 lakh per transaction (₹5 lakh for specific categories)
- **Fee**: Zero for users (charges apply for merchants over ₹2,000)
- **Major Apps**: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM
- **Daily Limit**: Varies by bank (typically ₹1-5 lakh)

### NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer)
- Batch-based settlement (every half hour)
- No minimum or maximum limit
- Available 24x7 (since December 2019)
- No charges by most banks for online NEFT

### RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement)
- Real-time settlement for high-value transactions
- Minimum: ₹2 lakh
- Available 24x7
- Used for large transfers (property purchase, business payments)

### IMPS (Immediate Payment Service)
- Real-time transfer 24x7
- Limit: Up to ₹5 lakh
- Small fee applicable (₹5-15)
- Works even on holidays

## Financial Planning Basics

### Emergency Fund
- Keep 6 months of expenses in liquid form
- Split between savings account and liquid mutual fund
- Should cover: EMIs, rent, insurance premiums, daily expenses
- Don't invest emergency fund in stocks or locked instruments

### 50-30-20 Budget Rule
- **50% Needs**: Rent, groceries, EMIs, insurance, utility bills
- **30% Wants**: Dining out, entertainment, shopping, vacations
- **20% Savings/Investment**: PPF, mutual funds, FDs, NPS, emergency fund

### Debt Management
- Total EMI should not exceed 40% of net monthly income
- Pay off highest-interest debt first (credit card → personal loan → car loan → home loan)
- Never take a new loan to pay off an old one (unless consolidating at lower rate)
- Avoid using credit card for EMI purchases — interest rates are 24-42%

## Banking Complaints and Grievances
- First: Complain to bank's customer service
- If unresolved in 30 days: File complaint with Banking Ombudsman (RBI)
- Online portal: https://cms.rbi.org.in
- Banking Ombudsman can award compensation up to ₹20 lakh

# Home Loan Guide — India

## Overview
A home loan is a secured loan provided by banks and housing finance companies (HFCs) to purchase, construct, or renovate a residential property. The property itself serves as collateral.

## Eligibility Criteria
### For Salaried Individuals
- Age: 21-65 years
- Minimum salary: ₹25,000/month (varies by bank)
- Employment: At least 2 years of work experience, 1 year with current employer
- CIBIL Score: 750+ recommended (minimum 650)
- Loan-to-Value (LTV): Up to 80% of property value (90% for loans up to ₹30 lakh)

### For Self-Employed Individuals
- Age: 21-65 years
- Business vintage: Minimum 3 years
- Minimum annual income: ₹3-5 lakh (varies)
- ITR for last 3 years required
- CIBIL Score: 700+ recommended

## EMI Calculation
EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N – 1]
Where:
- P = Principal loan amount
- R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100)
- N = Loan tenure in months

## Documents Required
- Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
- Address proof
- Income proof (salary slips for 3 months, Form 16)
- Bank statements (6 months)
- Property documents
- Employment proof

## Tax Benefits
### Section 80C — Principal Repayment
- Deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh per year on principal repayment
- Available under old tax regime

### Section 24(b) — Interest Payment
- Deduction up to ₹2 lakh per year on interest paid for self-occupied property
- No limit for let-out property

### Section 80EEA — Additional Deduction
- Additional ₹1.5 lakh deduction on interest (for loans sanctioned between 1 April 2019 – 31 March 2022, property value up to ₹45 lakh)

## Risks to Consider
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Floating rate loans mean your EMI can increase if RBI raises the repo rate
- **Property Market Risk**: Property values can decrease, leading to negative equity
- **Job Loss Risk**: EMI burden during unemployment can lead to default
- **Over-leveraging**: Loan EMI should not exceed 40% of net monthly income
- **Hidden Charges**: Processing fees (0.5%-1%), prepayment penalty (for fixed rate), legal charges, valuation charges

## Tips for Home Loan Borrowers
1. Compare rates across at least 5 lenders
2. Negotiate processing fees — many banks waive them during festive seasons
3. Opt for floating rate — historically cheaper and no prepayment penalty
4. Make part-prepayments whenever you get a bonus or windfall
5. Choose shorter tenure if affordable — saves lakhs in interest
6. Get pre-approved before property hunting — stronger negotiation position
7. Read the loan agreement carefully before signing
